*Mahābhārata* (12.87.3).—(The same idea as Manu; the wording being
slightly different.)

*Āpastamba* (2.26.4, 5).—‘He shall appoint men of the first three
castes, who are pure and truthful over villages and towns, for the
protection of the people. Their servants shall possess the same
qualities.’

*Viṣṇu*. (3.7-10).—‘He shall appoint chiefs or governors in every
village;—also lords of every group of ten villages;—and lords of every
group of hundred villages and lords of a whole district.’

*Viṣṇudharmottara* (Vīramitrodaya-Rājanīti, p. 249).—‘He shall appoint
men to offices, high, middling and low; he shall appoint the lord of a
village, the lord of ten villages and the lord of hundred villages, as
also the lord of a province.’

*Āpastamba* (Vīramitrodaya-Rājanīti, p. 949).—‘In villages and in cities
he shall appoint high-horn, pure and truthful men for the protection of
the people.’

*Śukranīti* (1.383-386).—‘A *grāma*, village, is that which has an area
of one *kroṣa* and whose yield is 1,000 silver *karṣas*.’
